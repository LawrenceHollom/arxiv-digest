### 1. A combinatorial large sieve for Sidon sets, distances, and norm forms

**Authors:** Ernie Croot, Junzhe Mao, Cosmin Pohoata, Adam Sheffer, Chi Hoi Yip  
**arXiv:** [2606.17487](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17487)

**What it claims to prove.**  
The paper develops a new combinatorial large sieve method for sets with bounded algebraic multiplicities, exploiting algebraic splitting modulo many small primes: local congruence branching produces many modular collisions, while global bounded-multiplicity hypotheses force these collisions to be rare. Three main results follow. (1) Every Sidon subset $A \subset \{1^2, \ldots, N^2\}$ satisfies $|A| \le N\exp(-c\log N/\log\log N)$, giving the first super-polylogarithmic saving for the classical Alon–Erdős problem. (2) The largest subset of $[N]^2$ with no repeated distance satisfies the same bound $N\exp(-c\log N/\log\log N)$, which is the first progress in over thirty years on a problem of Erdős and Guy; the same method gives a super-polylogarithmic saving for subsets of $[N]^2$ with no isosceles triangles, a problem recently popularized by Ellenberg and by Charton–Ellenberg–Wagner–Williamson. (3) An entropic extension yields bounds for $B_2[g]$-sets in the squares and, via norm forms over arbitrary number fields, the first nontrivial bounds for $B_3[g]$-sets in the cubes and $B_4[g]$-sets in the fourth powers.

**Significance.**  
The Sidon-in-squares problem and the grid repeated-distance problem are classical questions that had resisted any super-polylogarithmic improvement. Obtaining the first such savings for all of these problems in a single unified framework represents a genuine breakthrough in additive combinatorics and combinatorial number theory. The team — Croot (known for the cap-set bound), Pohoata and Sheffer (leading incidence-geometry researchers), and Yip — spans additive combinatorics, incidence geometry, and extremal combinatorics at the highest level. The extension to $B_k[g]$-sets in $k$-th power sets breaks open a further family of problems that had been completely inaccessible.

### 2. A Counterexample to Wegner's Conjecture for Axis-Parallel Rectangles

**Authors:** Deepak Ajwani, Rishikesh Gajjala, Rajiv Raman, Saurabh Ray  
**arXiv:** [2606.17854](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17854)

**What it claims to prove.**  
In 1965, Wegner conjectured that every finite family $\mathcal{R}$ of axis-parallel rectangles in the plane satisfies $\tau(\mathcal{R}) \le 2\nu(\mathcal{R}) - 1$, where $\tau$ is the minimum piercing number and $\nu$ is the maximum packing size. The paper gives an explicit counterexample: a triangle-free rectangle-intersection graph on $n$ vertices whose independence number is at most $n/4$. Since the graph is triangle-free, no piercing point lies in three rectangles, so every piercing point covers at most two rectangles and $\tau \ge n/2 \ge 2\nu$, contradicting Wegner's bound. A slightly more general construction achieves $\tau(\mathcal{R}) \ge 2.21\,\nu(\mathcal{R})$. As an immediate consequence, the standard LP (clique) relaxation for the Maximum Independent Set of Rectangles problem has integrality gap at least $2.21$.

**Significance.**  
Wegner's conjecture is a 60-year-old problem at the junction of combinatorial geometry and discrete optimisation. Over six decades it was verified for special classes of rectangles and the best known bounds on $\tau/\nu$ were progressively tightened, but the conjecture itself had never been settled. An explicit disproof — particularly one that additionally gives a quantitative lower bound on the ratio $\tau/\nu$ strictly exceeding 2 — definitively closes the problem and reshapes the theory of rectangle piercing. The integrality gap lower bound directly tightens the worst-case analysis of LP-based approximation algorithms for Maximum Independent Set of Rectangles, one of the central open problems in computational geometry.

### 3. The Erdős–Hajnal High-Girth Subgraph Conjecture Holds in the Polynomial Chromatic-Sparsity Regime

**Authors:** Eric Li (Trinity College, University of Cambridge)  
**arXiv:** [2606.17901](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17901)

**What it claims to prove.**  
For a graph $G$ and integer $r \ge 4$, put $h_r(G) = \max\{\chi(H) : H \subseteq G,\, \mathrm{girth}(H) \ge r\}$. Erdős and Hajnal asked whether $h_r(G) \to \infty$ as $\chi(G) \to \infty$ for every fixed $r \ge 4$. The paper proves this in every fixed polynomial edge-density regime: for all $r \ge 4$, $k \ge 2$, and $P, C > 0$, there is an explicit $M = M_{r,k}(P,C)$ such that $\chi(G) \ge M$ and $e(G) \le C\cdot\chi(G)^P$ together imply $h_r(G) \ge k$. The same conclusion extends to the quasi-polynomial range $e(G) \le \exp(C_0(\log\chi(G))^a)$ for $1 < a < 3/2$, and in each polynomial-density regime the bound $f_{P,C}(k,r) \le k^{O_{r,P,C}(1)}$ is obtained. The proof combines a chromatic-defect random extraction lemma, near-quadratic sparse-core bases, and a peeling/thinning bootstrap that increases the admissible edge exponent by $1/(r-1)$ at each step. A parallel fractional framework based on Mohar–Wu preservation is developed, and structural saturation results for potential counterexamples are established.

**Significance.**  
The Erdős–Hajnal high-girth conjecture asks whether high chromatic number forces a high-chromatic high-girth subgraph, a fundamental question in the structure-vs-property paradigm of extremal graph theory. The polynomial edge-density regime is natural and encompasses many important graph-theoretic settings. The result is the first resolution of the conjecture in any regime that goes significantly beyond previously known special cases. The structural saturation results — including Moore-strength exact-cycle packings and quadratic saturation in colour-pair space — and the fractional surgery framework are novel technical contributions of independent interest.

### 4. The independence number of uncrowded hypergraphs: bounds matching the shattering threshold

**Authors:** Abhishek Dhawan, Abhishek Methuku, Minh-Quan Vo  
**arXiv:** [2606.18048](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18048)

**What it claims to prove.**  
A $k$-uniform hypergraph is *uncrowded* if no two hyperedges share two vertices. The Ajtai–Komlós–Pintz–Spencer–Szemerédi (AKPSS) theorem guarantees an independent set of size $c_k n (\log\Delta/\Delta)^{1/(k-1)}$ for some $c_k > 0$ in every $n$-vertex $k$-uniform uncrowded hypergraph of maximum degree $\Delta$. The optimal value of $c_k$ is a major open problem. The paper proves that uncrowded hypergraphs attain the *shattering-threshold constant* $(1/(k-1))^{1/(k-1)}$: for every $\varepsilon > 0$ and $k \ge 2$, every $k$-uniform uncrowded hypergraph of sufficiently large maximum degree $\Delta$ contains an independent set of size at least $(1-\varepsilon)\,n\bigl(\tfrac{\log\Delta}{(k-1)\Delta}\bigr)^{1/(k-1)}$. As a corollary, a conjecture of Verstraëte and Wilson is resolved: the same bound (up to a $1-o_k(1)$ factor) holds for $k$-uniform linear hypergraphs. The algorithms are constructive with $\tilde{O}(n\Delta)$ randomised static time and $\tilde{O}(1)$ LOCAL rounds.

**Significance.**  
The AKPSS theorem is one of the benchmarks of probabilistic combinatorics, and determining its optimal constant has been open since the 1980s. The shattering threshold constant arises from the solution-space geometry of random constraint-satisfaction problems and from statistical physics, so the fact that these two contexts share the same critical constant is a deep and surprising structural statement. The result resolves two conjectures simultaneously, yields efficient algorithms in both static and distributed models, and extends cleanly from uncrowded to linear hypergraphs. Methuku is a leading researcher in extremal combinatorics with a strong track record across Turán-type and Ramsey-type problems.

### 5. Universal probability bounds for partial Latin squares

**Authors:** Jack Allsop, Patrick Morris  
**arXiv:** [2606.18174](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18174)

**What it claims to prove.**  
The paper proves that if $\alpha, \beta > 0$ satisfy $2\alpha + \beta < 1$, then there exist positive constants $\delta = \delta(\alpha,\beta)$ and $\Delta = \Delta(\alpha,\beta)$ such that for any partial Latin square $P$ of order $n$ with $k = k(n)$ non-empty cells occupying at most $\alpha n$ rows and $\beta n$ columns, the probability that a uniformly random Latin square of order $n$ contains $P$ lies between $(\delta/n)^k$ and $(\Delta/n)^k$. Two applications are given: (1) the expected number of order-$3$ subsquares in a random Latin square of order $n$ is non-vanishing as $n \to \infty$ — the first proof of this basic fact; (2) the best known asymptotics for the expected number of order-$a$ subsquares in a random order-$n$ Latin square are obtained for all $2 < a = o(n^{1/2})$. The results also have implications for completions of partial Latin squares.

**Significance.**  
Latin squares are among the most fundamental combinatorial objects, with connections to finite geometry, coding theory, and experimental design. The question of which substructures appear in a random Latin square, and at what rate, has attracted sustained attention but remained elusive outside the uniform case. The two-sided probability bounds proved here are essentially tight in the given regime, and they resolve the long-open question of whether order-$3$ subsquares persist asymptotically. The framework extends naturally to completions, an active research direction following Keevash's proof of the existence of designs. Allsop and Morris are productive researchers in probabilistic combinatorics with previous work on Latin squares and combinatorial designs.

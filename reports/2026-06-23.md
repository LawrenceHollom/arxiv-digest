### 1. Determining decomposition thresholds for long odd cycles

**Authors:** Bertille Granet, Daniel Horsley  
**arXiv:** [2606.21548](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21548)

**What it claims to prove.**  
An $\ell$-cycle decomposition of a graph $G$ is a partition of $E(G)$ into $\ell$-cycles. The $\ell$-cycle decomposition threshold $\delta_{C_\ell}$ is the least real number such that any sufficiently large $n$-vertex graph with minimum degree at least $(\delta_{C_\ell}+o(1))n$ admits an $\ell$-cycle decomposition if and only if $\ell \mid |E(G)|$ and every vertex has even degree. Nash-Williams' conjecture, recently resolved by Delcourt and Postle, asserts $\delta_{C_3} = 3/4$. Glock, Kühn, and Osthus subsequently posed the problem of determining $\delta_{C_\ell}$ for larger odd $\ell$; a natural generalization of Nash-Williams' conjecture predicts $\delta_{C_\ell} = \frac{\ell}{2\ell-2}$ for all odd $\ell \geq 3$. This paper proves that this formula is correct for all odd $\ell \geq 73$.

**Significance.**  
The Delcourt–Postle resolution of Nash-Williams' triangle conjecture was one of the landmark combinatorics results of the past decade. The natural follow-up — determining whether the same minimum-degree threshold formula governs all odd cycles — has been open since the question was posed by Glock, Kühn, and Osthus. Establishing $\delta_{C_\ell} = \frac{\ell}{2\ell-2}$ for all large odd $\ell$ confirms the generalized Nash-Williams picture and provides the first systematic determination of decomposition thresholds beyond triangles. Granet and Horsley are leading researchers in combinatorial design theory and graph decompositions, and their work builds on absorption and regularity techniques that have transformed graph factorisation theory. The remaining cases $\ell \in \{5, 7, \ldots, 71\}$ are now the only gap.

### 2. Spectral Gap for the Binary Fixed-Margin Swap Chain

**Authors:** Weibo Fu, Qian Qin, Guanyang Wang  
**arXiv:** [2606.22636](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.22636)

**What it claims to prove.**  
The lazy swap chain on $m \times n$ binary matrices with prescribed row and column sums is a classical Markov chain sampler used in ecology, statistics, and network analysis. The paper proves that its spectral gap is at least $\binom{m}{2}^{-1}\binom{n}{2}^{-1}$, which is tight in the worst case. This establishes the inverse-polynomial mixing bound conjectured by Kannan, Tetali, and Vempala in 1997 for arbitrary feasible margins. The proof compares the swap chain with a two-row heat-bath chain, reduces to the three-row case, and analyses the resulting three-row inequality by decomposing functions according to the column-count variable and Johnson harmonic sectors.

**Significance.**  
The Kannan–Tetali–Vempala (KTV) conjecture on rapid mixing of the fixed-margin swap chain has been open for 29 years and is one of the central unsolved problems in the theory of Markov chain sampling. A polynomial spectral gap immediately gives a polynomial-time sampler for the uniform distribution on binary contingency tables with arbitrary margins, resolving a fundamental bottleneck for exact tests in statistics and null-model analyses in network science. The authors note that the proof strategy — including the comparison with the two-row heat-bath chain, the reduction to three rows, and the Johnson harmonic sector decomposition — was initially proposed by ChatGPT 5.5 Pro; the human authors verified the arguments, rewrote the proof, and take mathematical responsibility for the final form. Regardless of how the strategy was generated, if the proof is correct this is a landmark resolution of a long-standing conjecture at the interface of combinatorics, probability, and algorithms.

### 3. Non-acyclic spaces of line transversals

**Authors:** Haochi Jiang, Martin Tancer  
**arXiv:** [2606.23193](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23193)

**What it claims to prove.**  
Cheong, Goaoc, and Holmsen conjectured that every connected component of the space of line transversals to a finite family of pairwise disjoint open convex sets in $\mathbb{R}^d$ is acyclic. The paper disproves this conjecture: for every $n \geq 1$, there exists a finite family of pairwise disjoint open convex sets in $\mathbb{R}^{3n}$ such that the $(n-1)$st homology group of the space of line transversals is nonzero over an arbitrary ring. In particular, in sufficiently high dimensions the space of line transversals can have arbitrarily high non-trivial homology.

**Significance.**  
The Cheong–Goaoc–Holmsen conjecture was a central problem in discrete and combinatorial geometry, predicting a topological regularity for line transversal spaces that would parallel classical Helly-type theorems. Acyclicity of such spaces was seen as a geometric consequence of convexity, and would have had implications for visibility algorithms and depth problems in computational geometry. The disproof reveals that the topology of line transversal spaces is fundamentally richer than that of intersection patterns in classical Helly theory, and raises the question of which weaker topological properties remain provable. Martin Tancer is a leading expert in topological combinatorics, known for deep results on embeddability and intersection complexes.

### 4. A sharp product bound for non-trivial cross-intersecting families

**Authors:** Yang Huang  
**arXiv:** [2606.23322](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23322)

**What it claims to prove.**  
Two families $\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B} \subseteq \binom{[n]}{k}$ are cross-intersecting if $A \cap B \neq \emptyset$ for all $A \in \mathcal{A}$, $B \in \mathcal{B}$, and non-trivial if neither family is a star. Pyber proved $|\mathcal{A}||\mathcal{B}| \leq \binom{n-1}{k-1}^2$ in general; Frankl, and independently Frankl–Wang, conjectured that requiring non-triviality of both families forces $|\mathcal{A}||\mathcal{B}| \leq h(n,k)^2$, where $h(n,k) = \binom{n-1}{k-1} - \binom{n-k-1}{k-1} + 1$ is the size of the Hilton–Milner family. This paper proves the conjecture in full generality for $n \geq 2k$ and $k \geq 3$, and completely characterises all extremal pairs: when $n > 2k$ the two families are isomorphic, and when $n = 2k$ they are complement-dual. The paper also disproves a related conjecture of Frankl and Wang.

**Significance.**  
Cross-intersecting families are a fundamental object in extremal set theory, and the non-trivial product bound is the natural analogue of the Hilton–Milner theorem for this symmetric product setting. Earlier partial results covered only $k = 3$ and the range $k \geq 8$, $n \geq 4k$; the full resolution for all valid parameters is a significant completion of this theory. The sharp characterisation of extremal pairs — forcing a symmetric-or-dual structure, in contrast to the asymmetric extremisers for the sum problem — reveals a qualitative difference between sum and product optimisation in cross-intersecting combinatorics. The proof introduces new properties of an extended shift operation and a diversity technique, tools likely to find further application.

### 5. Beating the Ahlswede–Khachatrian bound for the Erdős–Frankl–Pach problem

**Authors:** Tuan Tran, Zixiang Xu  
**arXiv:** [2606.23469](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23469)

**What it claims to prove.**  
In the 1980s, Erdős and, independently, Frankl and Pach conjectured that for sufficiently large $n$, every $(d+1)$-uniform family on $[n]$ with VC-dimension at most $d$ has size at most $\binom{n-1}{d}$, the size of a star. Ahlswede and Khachatrian disproved this in 1997 with a family of size $\binom{n-1}{d} + \binom{n-4}{d-2}$, after which this value was widely believed to be the true maximum, a belief formalised as the Mubayi–Zhao conjecture (2007) and further supported by Wang–Xu–Zhang's 2026 proof of the case $d=2$. This paper disproves the Mubayi–Zhao conjecture for every $d \geq 3$ by constructing $(d+1)$-uniform families of VC-dimension at most $d$ that are larger than the Ahlswede–Khachatrian bound. The constructions indicate that the true extremal value depends delicately on both $n$ and $d$.

**Significance.**  
The Mubayi–Zhao conjecture was one of the most concrete and widely-tested predictions in the extremal theory of VC-dimension-bounded families, having withstood scrutiny for nearly two decades. Its disproof for all $d \geq 3$ is a genuine surprise: the AK construction had remained unbeaten since 1997, and the very recent verification of the conjecture for $d=2$ appeared to strengthen the case for its truth in general. (An independent simultaneous disproof for $d \geq 3$ via a lifting construction appears in arXiv:2606.22064 by Zhao and Ge.) The revelation that the problem is $n$-and-$d$-sensitive opens a rich new landscape of questions and undermines the dominant heuristic for what extremal VC-bounded families look like. Tuan Tran is a prominent researcher in extremal combinatorics with a track record of resolving major conjectures.

### 1. A sharp *p*-biased product bound for *r*-cross-intersecting families

**Authors:** Fan Chang, Hong Liu, Miao Liu  
**arXiv:** [2606.15618](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15618)

**What it claims to prove.**  
Let $\mathcal{F}_1, \ldots, \mathcal{F}_r \subseteq 2^{[n]}$ be $r$-cross-intersecting families (every $F_i \in \mathcal{F}_i$ and $F_j \in \mathcal{F}_j$ share at least one element). The paper proves the sharp product inequality
$$\prod_{i=1}^{r} \mu_p(\mathcal{F}_i) \le p^r$$
in the $p$-biased measure for all $r \ge 2$ and $0 \le p \le \frac{r-1}{r}$, with equality exactly at a common $1$-star. This confirms the equal-bias case of a longstanding conjecture of Frankl and Tokushige, and for $r = 3$ fully confirms a conjecture of Tokushige. A companion stability theorem shows that every near-extremal $r$-tuple is close in $p$-biased measure to a common $1$-star, with an optimal linear dependence on the product deficit.

**Significance.**  
The Frankl–Tokushige conjecture is one of the central open problems in extremal set theory, generalising the Erdős–Ko–Rado theorem. Previous results required additional hypotheses or handled only special values of $r$. This paper achieves the sharp inequality with the correct range of $p$, uses a coupling argument at the critical bias together with an isoperimetric inequality, and adds a stability result with optimal constants — a combination that places it among the strongest recent results in the area. Hong Liu is one of the most active and influential combinatorialists currently working; his results consistently appear in the highest-tier journals.

### 2. A sharp bound on the integrality gap in the 3-set cover problem

**Authors:** Eli Berger, Ron Holzman  
**arXiv:** [2606.16921](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16921)

**What it claims to prove.**  
Given a hypergraph with edges of size at most 3, the *3-set cover problem* asks for the minimum number of hyperedges covering all vertices. The paper proves that the integrality gap — the ratio between the optimal integral and fractional (LP) solutions — is at most $\frac{3}{2}$, and that this bound is tight. This improves the classical Lovász bound of $\frac{11}{6}$ and a subsequent bound of $\frac{5}{3}$ by Fujito and Okumura. As corollaries: every 3-uniform regular hypergraph on $n$ vertices can be covered by $\lfloor n/2 \rfloor$ edges (solving the $k=3$ case of a problem of de A. Moreira and Kohayakawa), and a variant of the Gale–Shapley stable marriage theorem for triples is derived.

**Significance.**  
Set cover is one of the fundamental NP-hard problems in combinatorial optimisation, and pinning down the exact integrality gap for the 3-uniform case — a problem that remained open through decades of work — is a genuine landmark. The sharpness of $\frac{3}{2}$ means no further improvement is possible via LP relaxation alone, closing the question definitively. The corollary on regular hypergraph coverings resolves a clean combinatorial problem that had resisted attack. Berger and Holzman are experienced researchers at the Technion with a track record on sharp combinatorial results.

### 3. Canonical Ramsey theorem for graphs with clean intersections

**Authors:** Matías Azócar Carvajal, Ayush Basu, Christian Reiher, Vojtěch Rödl, Giovanne Santos, Mathias Schacht  
**arXiv:** [2606.16955](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16955)

**What it claims to prove.**  
The classical canonical Ramsey theorem (Erdős–Rado) guarantees a canonically coloured copy of any graph $F$ in some large host $H$. The Nešetřil–Rödl theorem (1978) strengthened this by requiring the host to have certain structural properties. This paper proves that for every ordered graph $F$ there exists an ordered host $H$ and a system $\mathscr{H}_F$ of induced copies of $F$ with the following properties simultaneously: (1) every colouring of the edges of $H$ yields a canonically coloured member of $\mathscr{H}_F$; (2) any two copies in $\mathscr{H}_F$ intersect in at most a single vertex or edge (*clean intersections*); (3) $H$ has the same clique number as $F$; (4) $H$ has the same odd girth as $F$ when $F$ is non-bipartite; and (5) when $F$ is connected, the copies preserve pairwise distances in $H$.

**Significance.**  
Canonical Ramsey theory is a major subfield tracing to Erdős–Rado, and achieving all structural control properties simultaneously — particularly distance preservation for connected $F$ — has been a long-sought goal. The team includes Vojtěch Rödl (a central figure in Ramsey theory for 50 years, Wolf Prize winner) and Mathias Schacht (leading expert in extremal combinatorics), giving the result immediate credibility and visibility. Clean intersections as a structural tool open avenues for further applications in Ramsey multiplicity and density Ramsey theory.

### 4. Coarse Balanced Separators in Biclique-Induced-Minor-Free Graphs

**Authors:** Maria Chudnovsky, Julien Codsi, Claire Kaneshiro  
**arXiv:** [2606.14974](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14974)

**What it claims to prove.**  
*Coarse treewidth* is a recently-introduced robustification of treewidth where bags in a tree decomposition are covered by balls of bounded radius. Abrishami, Czyżewska, Kluk, Pilipczuk, Pilipczuk and Rążewski (2025) conjectured the following coarse analogue of the Robertson–Seymour separation theorem: if every vertex-weighted version of a graph $G$ admits a balanced separator coverable by boundedly many balls of radius $r$, then $G$ has a tree decomposition whose bags are coverable by boundedly many balls of some radius $r'$. This paper verifies a *stronger* variant of this conjecture for all $r$ for the hereditary class of $K_{t,t}$-induced-minor-free graphs of bounded clique number. The key technical result — shown to have independent interest — is that in such graphs, a large vertex set $Y$ can always be "broken" by a set $Z$ of size polynomial in $s$ (the clique-number bound), so that no ball of radius $r$ in $G - Z$ covers a large proportion of $Y$.

**Significance.**  
Treewidth and its generalisations underpin much of algorithmic graph theory and structural graph theory. Verifying a coarse analogue of the separator–treewidth equivalence for $K_{t,t}$-induced-minor-free graphs is a substantial step toward understanding coarse graph structure theory. Maria Chudnovsky is a MacArthur Fellow and one of the foremost structural graph theorists alive; her involvement signals a deep, polished result. This work builds on and complements the breakthrough progress on induced subgraphs made by Chudnovsky's group over the past decade.

### 5. Bounded Bruhat intervals and affine Coxeter groups

**Authors:** Grant T. Barkley, Christian Gaetz  
**arXiv:** [2606.16894](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16894)

**What it claims to prove.**  
Dyer (1991) proved that for each fixed length $k$, only finitely many isomorphism types of Bruhat intervals of length $k$ appear in finite Coxeter groups. This paper proves that the same holds for *affine* Coxeter groups, and moreover that this property characterises affine groups among all infinite Coxeter groups: an infinite Coxeter group has finitely many Bruhat interval types at each length if and only if it is affine. As a second characterisation, the paper shows that the coefficients of $q$ in the Kazhdan–Lusztig (KL) polynomials of a Coxeter group are bounded (uniformly over all pairs) if and only if the group is finite or affine.

**Significance.**  
Kazhdan–Lusztig polynomials are central objects connecting combinatorics, representation theory, and geometric Lie theory. Bounding their coefficients — equivalently, characterising when the KL $q$-power series stays globally finite — has been a question of considerable interest since the KL polynomials were introduced in 1979. The clean dichotomy (finite or affine ↔ bounded) is striking and likely to be widely applied. The result on Bruhat intervals simultaneously resolves the natural structural question of when the local combinatorics of the Bruhat order stabilises. Barkley (Harvard PhD, now postdoc) and Gaetz (Harvard/Cornell) are rising stars in algebraic combinatorics with strong track records; this appears to be their most impactful result to date.

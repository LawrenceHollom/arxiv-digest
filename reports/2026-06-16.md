## 1. Super-Arrhenius relaxation of the triangular plaquette model in any dimension

**Authors:** Laurent Bartholdi, Ivailo Hartarsky, Ivan Mitrofanov  
**arXiv:** [2606.16259](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16259)  
**Primary category:** math.PR (cross-posted to cond-mat.stat-mech, math.CO, math.DS, math.GR)

### What it claims to prove

Consider the triangular lattice plaquette model: lamps sit at vertices, switches at even vertices of the dual hexagonal lattice, and each switch toggles its three incident lamps. The Glauber dynamics at inverse temperature β has long been expected to exhibit super-Arrhenius ("fragile glass") relaxation, with relaxation time scaling as e^{β²}. Newman and Moore conjectured this e^{β²} scaling in 1999. The paper proves, for *all dimensions d ≥ 2*, that

$$e^{\beta^2/C}/C \;\le\; T_{\mathrm{rel}} \;\le\; C\,e^{e^{C\beta}},$$

establishing the lower bound matching the conjectured scaling. On tori of side 2^k (with k/β → 0), they sharpen this to T_rel = e^{2βk(1+o(1))}, confirming the e^{β²} scaling in the natural thermodynamic limit. As a byproduct they prove uniqueness of the Gibbs measure, obtain consequences for the word-problem complexity of the Baumslag finitely-presented group, and derive ergodic-theory corollaries about the Ledrappier subshift. The main engine is new results in extremal and enumerative combinatorics together with renormalisation ideas for the dynamics.

### Significance and context

The triangular plaquette model is one of the canonical kinetically constrained models studied as a mathematical surrogate for glass-forming liquids. The e^{β²} scaling conjecture of Newman–Moore has been open for 27 years and is the plaquette-model analogue of the celebrated East-model result. Proving it in all dimensions via purely combinatorial methods — extremal combinatorics and a renormalisation scheme — is a methodological surprise: the proof technique is as interesting as the result. The crossover from polynomial to super-Arrhenius relaxation on non-periodic finite domains (T_rel grows as e^{β · n^{Θ(1)}} for n ≤ e^{β/C}) is a genuinely new phenomenon with no prior analogue. The Baumslag-group and Ledrappier-subshift applications illustrate breadth rarely seen in a combinatorics paper.

**Expected journal tier:** *Annals of Probability* or *Annals of Mathematics* (top-tier probability/mathematics journal). The depth of techniques across four mathematical fields places this firmly at the highest level.

## 2. A sharp bound on the integrality gap in the 3-set cover problem

**Authors:** Eli Berger, Ron Holzman  
**arXiv:** [2606.16921](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16921)  
**Primary category:** math.CO

### What it claims to prove

Given a hypergraph with edges of size at most 3, the 3-set cover LP relaxation provides a lower bound. This paper proves the integrality gap is at most **3/2**, which is best possible. The history of this bound is:

- Lovász (classical): gap ≤ 11/6,  
- Fujito–Okumura: gap ≤ 5/3,  
- This paper: gap = **3/2** (tight).

A corollary is that the vertex set of any 3-uniform *regular* hypergraph on n vertices can be covered by at most n/2 edges, solving the k = 3 case of a problem of de A. Moreira and Kohayakawa. A further application yields a variant of the Gale–Shapley stable-marriage theorem for triples.

### Significance and context

The integrality gap of set-cover relaxations is a central question sitting at the intersection of combinatorial optimisation and extremal combinatorics. The Lovász bound dated from the mid-1970s and the 5/3 improvement was already seen as near-optimal. Closing the gap all the way to 3/2 — and proving this is sharp — is a definitive resolution of the problem. The regular-hypergraph corollary (n/2 edges suffice) has a clean geometric flavour and the Gale–Shapley-for-triples application hints at broader structural consequences. Berger and Holzman are senior figures in combinatorics; their short, clean proofs tend to have lasting influence.

**Expected journal tier:** *Combinatorica* or *Journal of Combinatorial Theory, Series B* (top specialist combinatorics journals). The optimality and breadth of corollaries make acceptance essentially certain at this level.

## 3. A sharp p-biased product bound for r-cross-intersecting families

**Authors:** Fan Chang, Hong Liu, Miao Liu  
**arXiv:** [2606.15618](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15618)  
**Primary category:** math.CO

### What it claims to prove

For r ≥ 2, p ∈ [0, (r−1)/r], and r-cross-intersecting families F₁, …, F_r ⊆ 2^{[n]} (meaning every transversal has a common element), the paper proves

$$\prod_{i=1}^r \mu_p(\mathcal{F}_i) \;\le\; p^r,$$

with equality iff all families are a common 1-star, and with the range of p being optimal. Specialising to the uniform (equal-bias) case settles the **Frankl–Tokushige conjecture** (equal-bias version); for r = 3 it confirms a separate **conjecture of Tokushige**. A stability theorem is also proved: for r ≥ 3 every near-extremal r-tuple is close (in p-biased measure) to a common 1-star, with optimal linear dependence on the product deficit. The extremal proof uses a coordinatewise coupling at the critical bias and an isoperimetric inequality; the stability proof uses Fourier concentration via the biased Friedgut–Kalai–Naor theorem.

### Significance and context

Frankl and Tokushige have been central figures in extremal set theory since the 1980s, and their conjecture on biased measures is one of the cleanest open problems in the field. The cross-intersecting setting generalises both the Erdős–Ko–Rado theorem (r = 2) and its biased analogues. Settling the equal-bias Frankl–Tokushige conjecture with a matching stability theorem is a complete package: it characterises the extremal configurations and quantifies how much near-extremal families can deviate. The techniques — biased Fourier analysis, the FKN theorem, isoperimetry — are among the sharpest in the modern toolkit.

**Expected journal tier:** *Advances in Mathematics* or *Journal of Combinatorial Theory, Series A*. A solved conjecture with optimal stability is exactly what top combinatorics journals publish.

## 4. Bounded Bruhat intervals and affine Coxeter groups

**Authors:** Grant T. Barkley, Christian Gaetz  
**arXiv:** [2606.16894](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16894)  
**Primary category:** math.CO (cross-posted to math.RT)

### What it claims to prove

Dyer (1991) proved that for each fixed length k, only finitely many isomorphism types of length-k Bruhat intervals occur in *finite* Coxeter groups. This paper proves:

1. The same finiteness holds for all **affine Coxeter groups**.
2. Conversely, this property *characterises* affine groups among all infinite Coxeter groups: no other infinite Coxeter group has finitely many interval types of each length.
3. The coefficients of q in the **Kazhdan–Lusztig polynomials** of a Coxeter group are bounded if and only if the group is finite or affine.

The paper is short (a "short paper"), suggesting the proofs are elegant and structural rather than computational.

### Significance and context

Bruhat order and Kazhdan–Lusztig (KL) polynomials are fundamental objects in algebraic combinatorics and representation theory. KL polynomials carry information about singularities of Schubert varieties and Jantzen filtrations of Verma modules, so bounding their coefficients has direct geometric and representation-theoretic meaning. The fact that finite and affine Coxeter groups are *exactly* the groups with bounded KL coefficients is a beautiful characterisation that would have been difficult to predict. It places affine groups — which already enjoy a special role in Lie theory — at precisely the boundary where the combinatorics remains controlled. Barkley and Gaetz are known for earlier work clarifying the combinatorics of Coxeter groups.

**Expected journal tier:** *Advances in Mathematics*, *Compositio Mathematica*, or *Duke Mathematical Journal*. The algebraic significance and clean characterisation merit a high-tier algebra/combinatorics journal.

## 5. The number of cycles of a given length in dense hamiltonian graphs: proving Hilton's conjecture

**Authors:** Chengli Li, Leyou Xu, Bo Zhou  
**arXiv:** [2606.16114](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16114)  
**Primary category:** math.CO

### What it claims to prove

Sheehan (1977) proved that any hamiltonian graph of order n with more than ⌊n²/4⌋ + 1 edges contains at least two cycles of *every* length ℓ with 3 ≤ ℓ ≤ n. In the same paper Sheehan recorded a conjecture of Hilton strengthening this: such a graph contains at least **n − ℓ + 2** cycles of length ℓ. The paper proves Hilton's conjecture for all hamiltonian graphs of order at least 440.

### Significance and context

Hilton's conjecture sat unresolved for nearly 50 years. The extremal condition (edge density just above the Turán threshold for bipartite graphs plus a Hamiltonian cycle) is a natural and well-studied setting in extremal graph theory. The bound n − ℓ + 2 is tight: it matches for complete graphs minus a matching. Proving it essentially completely (for n ≥ 440, with finitely many exceptions checkable in principle) is a definitive resolution. The techniques likely involve a combination of stability methods and careful structural analysis of dense Hamiltonian graphs. This belongs to a line of work descending from results of Corrádi, Hajnal–Szemerédi, and Dirac.

**Expected journal tier:** *Journal of Combinatorial Theory, Series B* or *European Journal of Combinatorics*. A clean resolution of a 1977 conjecture in graph theory is well within the scope of the top graph-theory journals.

## Honourable mention

- **"Large Independent Sets in Flag Spheres"** (2606.16109, Shah): Disproves a conjecture of Chudnovsky and Nevo by constructing 3-dimensional flag simplicial spheres with independent sets asymptotically equal in size to the vertex set. A clean counterexample to a recent conjecture in topological combinatorics.
- **"Conjugacy Class Averages and Sidorenko's Conjecture"** (2606.15368, Zhao): Gives a new reduction of Sidorenko's conjecture (one of the most prominent open problems in extremal graph theory) to Cayley-type hosts via conjugacy averaging, and proves Sidorenko-type inequalities for 1-subdivision graphs on conjugacy-averaged kernels.
- **"Coarse Balanced Separators in Biclique-Induced-Minor-Free Graphs"** (2606.14974, Chudnovsky, Codsi, Kaneshiro): Verifies a strong variant of the 2025 Abrishami–Czyzewska–Kluk–Pilipczuk–Rzazewski coarse-treewidth conjecture for K_{t,t}-induced-minor-free graphs.

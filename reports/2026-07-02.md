### 1. On a moment determinacy conjecture of Bertoin and Yor

**Authors:** Martin Minchev  
**arXiv:** [2607.00132](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00132)

**Abstract.**  
> Let $\xi$ be an unkilled real-valued L\'evy process which drifts to $+\infty$ and has positive exponential moments of all orders, and define $I_\xi=\int_0^\infty e^{-\xi_t}\,dt$, and its reciprocal $X_\xi=1/I_\xi$. Bertoin and Yor proved that $X_\xi$ is moment-determinate when $\xi$ has no positive jumps, and conjectured that this condition is also necessary. We prove the latter.
> The proof is based on a lower bound near zero for the law of $I_\xi$. We show that a group of sufficiently many positive jumps near the origin puts $I_\xi$ on a suitable small scale. The first selected jump time is used as a one-dimensional smooth coordinate, yielding an absolutely continuous subcomponent of the law of $I_\xi$. After the change of variables, the resulting subdensity of $X_\xi$ satisfies a Krein moment indeterminacy criterion.

**What it claims to prove.**  
For an unkilled real-valued Lévy process $\xi$ drifting to $+\infty$ with positive exponential moments of all orders, consider the exponential functional $I_\xi=\int_0^\infty e^{-\xi_t}\,dt$ and its reciprocal $X_\xi=1/I_\xi$. Bertoin and Yor showed that $X_\xi$ is moment-determinate (uniquely recoverable from its moments) whenever $\xi$ has no positive jumps, and conjectured that the absence of positive jumps is also necessary. This paper proves the necessity direction, yielding a full if-and-only-if characterization. The argument produces a lower bound for the density of $I_\xi$ near zero by isolating a cluster of positive jumps close to the origin, uses the time of the first such jump as a smooth coordinate to extract an absolutely continuous component of the law of $I_\xi$, and then verifies a Krein-type indeterminacy criterion for the resulting density of $X_\xi$.

**Significance.**  
Exponential functionals of Lévy processes are a classical object in probability with applications to self-similar Markov processes, generalized Ornstein–Uhlenbeck processes, and mathematical finance (e.g., Asian option pricing). Bertoin and Yor's moment-determinacy results from the early 2000s are foundational in this area, and their necessity conjecture had remained a natural loose end. The paper closes the gap with a genuinely new construction — an explicit near-zero density lower bound obtained via jump clustering — rather than a routine extension of the original argument. It is a well-executed, single-author resolution of one specific, well-defined conjecture in a mature subfield, rather than a paper that reshapes the broader theory.

**Rating: C**

### 2. Critical Zeros and Unconditional Mean Value Theorems for twisted $\mathrm{PGL}(2)$ and $\mathrm{PGL}(3)$ $L$-functions

**Authors:** Brian Conrey, Chung-Hang Kwan, Yongxiao Lin, Caroline L. Turnage-Butterbaugh  
**arXiv:** [2607.00282](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00282)

**Abstract.**  
> Let $\Pi_{0}$ be a cuspidal automorphic representation of $\mathrm{PGL}_{3}(\mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{Q}})$. In this paper, we use Levinson's method to prove that, as $Q\to \infty$, at least $1/9$ of the zeros of the $L$-functions $L(s, \Pi_{0}\,\times\, \chi)$ lie on the critical line, where $\chi$ ranges over the family of primitive Dirichlet characters of conductor up to $Q$. This result is unconditional when $\Pi_{0}$ is self-dual, and otherwise holds under a mild condition.
> The key technical input is a new asymptotic formula with a power-saving error term for the mean square of the product of $L(s, \Pi_{0}\times \chi)$ and a Dirichlet polynomial with arbitrary coefficients in both the $T$- and $Q$-aspects for the range $Q^{\epsilon}\le T \le Q^{1/3-\epsilon}$. When $T=Q^{\epsilon}$, our asymptotic formula allows Dirichlet polynomials of length $\theta <1/2-\epsilon$; when $\theta=0$, it gives a strong error term of size $O_{\epsilon}(Q^{7/4+\epsilon})$. Furthermore, our result provides evidence for the CFKRS conjectures for large twists and large vertical shifts.
> We also obtain corresponding results for $\mathrm{PGL}_{2}(\mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{Q}})$, which are fully unconditional, quantitatively stronger, and also appear to be new.
> This work develops a refined, flexible, and uniform version of the Asymptotic Large Sieve for $L$-functions that does not require any unproven progress toward the Generalized Ramanujan Conjecture. The arithmetic of $\Pi_{0}$ plays a crucial and delicate role in our argument. This work also makes extensive use of Mathematica to handle various elaborate Hecke algebra computations. Our mean value theorem is readily applicable to many other problems in analytic number theory.

**What it claims to prove.**  
For a cuspidal automorphic representation $\Pi_0$ of $\mathrm{PGL}_3$, the authors use Levinson's method — the classical technique for establishing positive-proportion critical-line results — to show that at least $1/9$ of the zeros of the twisted $L$-functions $L(s,\Pi_0\times\chi)$, as $\chi$ ranges over primitive Dirichlet characters of conductor up to $Q$, lie on the critical line as $Q\to\infty$; the result is unconditional when $\Pi_0$ is self-dual and holds under a mild hypothesis otherwise. The key new tool is a uniform, power-saving asymptotic mean-value formula for $L(s,\Pi_0\times\chi)$ against an arbitrary Dirichlet polynomial, valid across a wide range of the $T$- and $Q$-aspects, obtained via a refined "Asymptotic Large Sieve" that avoids leaning on the Generalized Ramanujan Conjecture. They also derive stronger, fully unconditional analogues for $\mathrm{PGL}_2$.

**Significance.**  
Proving positive-proportion critical-line results for families of $L$-functions is a long and celebrated tradition going back to Levinson's original work on the Riemann zeta function and its extensions by Conrey, Iwaniec, Soundararajan and others; extending this machinery unconditionally to a genuinely degree-3 ($\mathrm{GL}(3)$) family, without relying on the Generalized Ramanujan Conjecture, is a substantial technical achievement given how much harder $\mathrm{GL}(3)$ mean-value estimates are than their $\mathrm{GL}(2)$ or $\mathrm{GL}(1)$ counterparts. Brian Conrey is one of the pioneers of this line of research, and the resulting "Asymptotic Large Sieve" is presented as broadly reusable for other analytic number theory problems. That said, the specific numerical proportion ($1/9$) is modest and far from anything resembling GRH, so despite its technical strength this paper is best read as an important addition to an already large body of critical-line literature rather than a conceptual breakthrough.

**Rating: B**

### 3. A Complete Intersection Theorem for Large Permutation Groups

**Authors:** Nathan Keller, Andrey Kupavskii, Noam Lifshitz, Ohad Sheinfeld  
**arXiv:** [2607.00318](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00318)

**Abstract.**  
> A family of permutations is called $t$-intersecting if any two permutations in the family agree on at least $t$ elements. We prove that there exists $n_0 \in \mathbb{N}$ such that for any $n>n_0$ and any $1 \leq t \leq n$, the maximum size of a $t$-intersecting family in $S_n$ is obtained by one of the families $\mathcal{F}_{n,t,r}=\{\sigma \in S_n: |\mathrm{Fixed}(\sigma) \cap \{1,2,\ldots,t+2r\}|\geq t+r\}$, where $\mathrm{Fixed}(\sigma)$ is the set of fixed points of $\sigma$. This proves an analogue of the classical Complete Intersection Theorem for large permutation groups, thus providing an essentially complete solution of the Deza-Frankl intersection problem for permutations (1977).

**What it claims to prove.**  
For families of permutations in $S_n$ that are $t$-intersecting (any two members agree on at least $t$ points), the paper shows that for $n$ larger than some threshold $n_0$ that does *not* depend on $t$, the extremal such families are always of the explicit "double-fix" type $\mathcal{F}_{n,t,r}$, consisting of permutations fixing at least $t+r$ points among a fixed set of $t+2r$ elements. This is a permutation-group analogue of the Ahlswede–Khachatrian Complete Intersection Theorem for set systems, and it resolves, for all $t$ simultaneously once $n$ is large, the Deza–Frankl intersection problem for permutations posed in 1977.

**Significance.**  
The Deza–Frankl problem is one of the oldest and best-known questions in the extremal theory of intersecting families, sitting alongside the Erdős–Ko–Rado theorem and its generalizations as a central pillar of the field. A large-$n$ analogue was previously known only for fixed $t$, notably via the influential work of Ellis, Friedgut and Pilpel using representation-theoretic and Fourier-analytic tools on $S_n$, leaving the uniform-in-$t$ regime open. Removing the dependence of the threshold $n_0$ on $t$, and thereby giving a single clean extremal description valid for essentially all $t$ at once, is a substantial unification, and the strength of the author list — Keller, Kupavskii and Lifshitz are all leading figures in modern extremal set theory and the analysis of Boolean and permutation functions — reflects the depth of technique required. The result is asymptotic in $n$ rather than exact for every $n$, so a fully finite Ahlswede–Khachatrian-style theorem for permutations remains open, but this is nonetheless a landmark, essentially complete resolution of a nearly 50-year-old problem.

**Rating: A**

### 4. The continuous oriented chromatic number of directed Schreier graphs of $\mathbb Z^2$-shift actions

**Authors:** Ruijun Wang  
**arXiv:** [2607.00367](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00367)

**Abstract.**  
> Let $\vec F(2^{\mathbb Z^2})$ be the directed Schreier graph on the free part of the Bernoulli shift $\mathbb Z^2\curvearrowright 2^{\mathbb Z^2}$, with arcs in the two coordinate directions. We prove that the continuous oriented chromatic number of it is 7, that is, there is a tournament on 7 vertices receiving a continuous graph homomorphism from $\vec F(2^{\mathbb Z^2})$ and there is no continuous graph homomorphism from $\vec F(2^{\mathbb Z^2})$ to any tournament on 6 vertices.

**What it claims to prove.**  
The paper considers the directed Schreier graph on the free part of the Bernoulli shift $\mathbb Z^2\curvearrowright 2^{\mathbb Z^2}$, with arcs given by the two coordinate generators, and computes its continuous oriented chromatic number exactly: the value is $7$. That is, there is a continuous graph homomorphism from this Schreier graph to some tournament on $7$ vertices, but no continuous homomorphism to any tournament on $6$ vertices.

**Significance.**  
This result sits within descriptive (Borel/continuous) combinatorics, a fast-growing area connecting Borel graph theory, distributed computing (the LOCAL model), and the topological dynamics of group actions, developed substantially over the last decade by researchers such as Marks, Bernshteyn and Grebík. Pinning down an exact chromatic-type parameter — rather than just upper and lower bounds — for a natural, canonical shift graph is a clean and technically satisfying contribution to this toolkit, and the specific value $7$ gives a concrete data point for oriented coloring problems, which are typically much harder to determine exactly than their undirected counterparts. The result is narrowly scoped to a single graph family and does not introduce a broadly applicable new technique, making it a solid, self-contained specialist contribution rather than an advance with wide ripple effects.

**Rating: C**

### 5. The Schur positivity of $\nabla m_\mu$

**Authors:** Dun Qiu, Minhao Zhang  
**arXiv:** [2607.00940](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00940)

**Abstract.**  
> Bergeron, Garsia, Haiman and Tesler conjectured in 1999 that, for all partitions $\mu,\lambda\vdash n$, the polynomial $(-1)^{|\mu|-\ell(\mu)}\langle \nabla m_\mu, s_\lambda\rangle$ has nonnegative integer coefficients, where $\nabla$ is the Bergeron–Garsia nabla operator, which acts diagonally on the modified Macdonald basis, and $m_\mu$ is the monomial symmetric function. In this article, we prove this conjecture, and more generally that $(-1)^{|\mu|-\ell(\mu)}\langle\nabla^r m_\mu,s_\lambda\rangle\in\mathbb{N}[q,t]$ for all $r\geq 1$. We establish a recursion showing that $(-1)^{|\mu|-\ell(\mu)}m_\mu$ has an expansion with coefficients in $\mathbb{Q}_{\geq 0}[q]$ in the symmetric functions $C_\alpha(1)$, where $C_a$ denotes the operator introduced by Haglund, Morse and Zabrocki. Combining this expansion with the compositional shuffle theorems of Carlsson–Mellit and Mellit, and with the Schur positivity of LLT polynomials, completes the proof. The same method, using the $e$-positivity of column LLT polynomials after the substitution $q\mapsto q+1$, also gives an $e$-positive analogue.

**What it claims to prove.**  
Bergeron, Garsia, Haiman and Tesler conjectured in 1999 that the coefficients of $\langle \nabla m_\mu, s_\lambda\rangle$, suitably signed, are always nonnegative integers, where $\nabla$ is the Bergeron–Garsia nabla operator acting on the modified Macdonald basis and $m_\mu$ is the monomial symmetric function. This paper proves the conjecture, and more generally establishes the analogous positivity for $\nabla^r m_\mu$ for every $r\geq 1$. The proof expands the signed monomial symmetric functions in terms of the Haglund–Morse–Zabrocki operators $C_\alpha(1)$ with nonnegative coefficients, then invokes the (already-proven) compositional shuffle theorems of Carlsson–Mellit and Mellit together with the Schur positivity of LLT polynomials to conclude; a parallel $e$-positivity statement follows via a $q\mapsto q+1$ substitution and column LLT $e$-positivity.

**Significance.**  
The nabla operator and Macdonald polynomial theory are central to modern algebraic combinatorics, most famously via the shuffle conjecture — proved by Carlsson–Mellit in 2015 and extended by Mellit — which describes the bigraded Frobenius characteristic of the ring of diagonal harmonics. The 1999 Bergeron–Garsia–Haiman–Tesler conjecture is a natural companion statement in the same circle of ideas that had remained open even after the shuffle theorem's resolution. This paper closes that gap by skillfully assembling the shuffle theorem and LLT positivity — both themselves major achievements of the last decade — with a new expansion identity, rather than by developing an entirely independent line of attack. It is a genuine and satisfying resolution of a well-known 25-year-old conjecture in a core area of algebraic combinatorics, though its reliance on recently established heavy machinery means it reads as the capstone of an existing program rather than the start of a new one.

**Rating: B**

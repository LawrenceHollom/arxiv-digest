### 1. Differential operators, anisotropy, and simplicial spheres

**Authors:** Kalle Karu, Matt Larson, Alan Stapledon  
**arXiv:** [2412.04561](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.04561)

**What it claims to prove.**  
The paper establishes identities involving differential operators in the generic artinian reduction of the Stanley–Reisner ring of a simplicial sphere, working in any positive characteristic. These identities are shown to be a consequence of a deeper identity on the degree map of the generic artinian reduction, which provides a unified structural source for all of them. Using these identities, the paper proves: (1) anisotropy of certain bilinear forms on the generic artinian reduction in any positive characteristic, generalising the characteristic-2 anisotropy that was the key algebraic ingredient in the Papadakis–Petrotou proof of the algebraic g-conjecture; and (2) weak Lefschetz results in any positive characteristic. The paper also shows that all of these identities and the resulting anisotropy and Lefschetz statements persist for the up Laplacian of an arbitrary finite simplicial complex in any dimension.

**Significance.**  
The g-conjecture for simplicial spheres — asserting that the $h$-vector of any simplicial sphere is an M-vector — was proved in characteristic 2 by Papadakis and Petrotou using specific characteristic-2 algebraic identities; a characteristic-free proof remains one of the central open problems in combinatorial commutative algebra. This paper removes the characteristic-2 restriction from the key algebraic engine of that proof, establishing the same anisotropy in all positive characteristics. If the remaining gap to characteristic 0 can be bridged, a complete characteristic-free proof of the algebraic g-conjecture would follow. Karu, Larson, and Stapledon are leading researchers in algebraic and topological combinatorics, with Larson and Stapledon having co-authored prior major results in the Lefschetz theory of simplicial complexes.

### 2. Probabilistic counting lemma for $K_4$

**Authors:** Warach Veeranonchai  
**arXiv:** [2603.29938](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29938)

**What it claims to prove.**  
The Gerke–Marciniszyn–Steger (GMS) conjecture — the sparse probabilistic counting lemma — states: for every fixed graph $H$ and every $\beta > 0$, there exists $\varepsilon > 0$ such that in a balanced blow-up of $H$ where each edge-class forms an $\varepsilon$-regular bipartite graph with exactly $m \gg n^{2-1/m_2(H)}$ edges, all but a $\beta^m$ fraction of such blow-ups contain at least $(1-\delta)$ times the expected number of copies of $H$. The paper establishes this conjecture for $H = K_4$.

**Significance.**  
The sparse Szemerédi regularity method has been one of the central tools in extremal graph theory for three decades. While the sparse embedding lemma (the KLR conjecture) was resolved in full generality by Conlon–Fox–Zhao, Saxton–Thomason, and Balogh–Morris–Samotij, the corresponding sparse probabilistic counting lemma has resisted all attempts beyond $K_3$. Proving the $K_4$ case requires handling the significantly more complex dependency structure that arises from triangles, and is the first genuinely new case. This paper is a substantial advance toward a complete resolution of the GMS conjecture, with direct implications for the probabilistic embedding of $K_4$-containing graphs in sparse random structures.

### 3. On the maximum density of $r$-graphs in which every $(r+1)$-set spans $0$ or $2$ edges

**Authors:** Vishesh Jain, Haoran Luo, Dhruv Mubayi  
**arXiv:** [2606.20367](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20367)

**What it claims to prove.**  
In 1984, Frankl and Füredi asked for the maximum edge density of an $n$-vertex $r$-uniform hypergraph ($r$-graph) in which every $(r+1)$-set of vertices spans exactly 0 or 2 edges. Their original construction yielded asymptotic density $2^{1-r}$, with the dependence on $r$ being exponentially small. This paper significantly improves this bound by constructing such $r$-graphs with edge density $\Omega(r^{-3})$, improving the dependence on $r$ from exponential to polynomial. The paper also establishes lower bounds for the more general problem in which every $(r+1)$-set spans an even number of edges from $\{0, 2, \ldots, 2k\}$.

**Significance.**  
The Frankl–Füredi problem is a natural and well-studied hypergraph Turán-type question with connections to additive combinatorics and extremal set theory. The forty-year gap between the $2^{1-r}$ construction and any polynomial lower bound is a striking one: moving from exponential to polynomial dependence represents a fundamental change in the qualitative picture and suggests that the true maximum density may in fact be polynomial in $1/r$. Dhruv Mubayi is one of the leading researchers in extremal hypergraph theory, with Vishesh Jain being a prominent probabilist and combinatorialist; together they bring the probabilistic and algebraic techniques needed for this construction.

### 4. Bipartite cuts in Ramsey-Turán style

**Authors:** József Balogh, Ce Chen, Bernard Lidický  
**arXiv:** [2606.20397](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20397)

**What it claims to prove.**  
The paper proves that every $K_5$-free $n$-vertex graph $G$ with sublinear independence number can be made bipartite by removing at most $n^2(1/18 + o(1))$ edges, and that the constant $1/18$ is best possible. The proof combines extensions of Turán's theorem to edge-weighted settings with a flag algebra computation.

**Significance.**  
Ramsey–Turán theory, initiated by Sós in the 1970s, studies graphs with a forbidden clique and a sublinear independence number, interpolating between the Turán and Ramsey problems. Computing the minimum number of edges whose removal makes such a graph bipartite — the bipartite edit distance in this restricted class — is a natural refinement that had not been determined exactly for $K_5$-free graphs. Obtaining the sharp constant $1/18$ via flag algebras brings a definitive resolution to this problem and adds to the growing body of exact Ramsey–Turán results. Balogh is one of the most prominent figures in extremal combinatorics, with a consistent record of exact and asymptotically tight results in this area.

### 5. Strong non-principality of positive codegree Turán density

**Authors:** Levente Bodnár, Jun Gao, Oleg Pikhurko, Mingyuan Rong, Shumin Sun  
**arXiv:** [2606.20494](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20494)

**What it claims to prove.**  
The minimum positive codegree $\delta^+_{k-1}(G)$ of a $k$-graph $G$ is the minimum, over all $(k-1)$-sets lying in at least one edge, of the number of edges containing that set. The positive codegree Turán density $\gamma^+(F)$ of a $k$-graph $F$ is the asymptotic maximum of $\delta^+_{k-1}(G)/n$ over all $F$-free $k$-graphs $G$ with $n \to \infty$ vertices. The paper proves that for every $k \geq 3$ there exist two $k$-graphs $F_1$ and $F_2$ such that
$$0 < \gamma^+(F_1, F_2) < \min\{\gamma^+(F_1), \gamma^+(F_2)\},$$
establishing a strong form of non-principality: forbidding both $F_1$ and $F_2$ together forces the positive codegree Turán density strictly below the minimum of the two individual densities, even when each individual density is strictly positive.

**Significance.**  
Principality — the property that the Turán density of a family is determined by the densities of its individual members — is a fundamental structural question in hypergraph Turán theory. For ordinary edge density, non-principality is easily exhibited but the phenomenon for codegree density is more subtle and surprising, since codegree conditions interact non-trivially with the hypergraph structure. Prior work had established non-principality only in weaker senses or for codegree density equal to zero; the strong form proved here, with strictly positive joint density, is a qualitatively stronger statement. Oleg Pikhurko is one of the world's leading researchers in extremal combinatorics, with major contributions to the flag algebra method and hypergraph Turán problems.
